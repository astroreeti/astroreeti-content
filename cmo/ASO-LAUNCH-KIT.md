## Why this matters more than it seems

Play Store search is a real discovery channel with zero cost — but only if the
listing is keyword-optimized *before* launch. Editing the title/keywords after
launch resets some ranking signal, so get this right on day one.

## App title (30 char limit — title carries the most ASO weight)

**`AstroReeti: Vedic Kundali AI`** (29 chars)

Alternates if that's taken or you want to test:
- `AstroReeti - Kundali & Dasha` (29 chars)
- `AstroReeti: Kundli, Muhurta` (28 chars)

Rationale: leads with brand (recall from Instagram), includes the #1 highest-
volume keyword "Kundali/Kundli" (support both spellings across metadata since
search behavior splits), signals AI + Vedic positioning distinctly from
generalist astrology apps.

## Short description (80 char limit)

## Screenshots & graphics (prepare before submission)

- First 2 screenshots matter most (shown in search results before tap) —
  lead with the Kundali chart visual and the AI-explanation UI, not the login
  screen.
- Include one screenshot each of: Kundali chart, Milan Vichar match score,
  Muhurta finder, Rashifal. Add short on-image captions in both Hindi and
  English (Play auto-shows localized listings but captions baked into images
  don't localize — consider two screenshot sets if bandwidth allows).
- Feature graphic (1024×500): brand + "AI Vedic Astrology" + the moon/mandala
  visual motif already established on Instagram, for cross-recognition.

## Launch-week plan (ratings velocity matters for ranking)

1. **Day 1:** Announce on Instagram (once IG is in "bridge" phase) and
   WhatsApp Channel with a direct Play Store link.
2. **Day 1–3:** Prompt happy users in-app (after a completed Kundali view,
   not on first open) to rate — Play's own guidance favors requesting reviews
   after a moment of value delivered, not immediately on install.
3. Respond to every review, especially the first 20–30 — Play Console reply
   activity is itself a positive signal and builds trust for later visitors.
4. Track category ranking and the Tier-1 keyword rankings weekly via Play
   Console's own "Search terms" report (visible after some install volume).

## Localization note (free win, given 20-language support)

Play Store lets you submit localized listings per language at no extra
technical cost since the app already supports 20 languages. At minimum,
prepare a **Hindi listing** (title, short & long description in Devanagari) —
this alone should meaningfully lift organic discovery for Hindi-language Play
Store search, which is a large and under-competed segment versus English
astrology-app search.
